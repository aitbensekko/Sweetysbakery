The tangy lemon curd relies on the coagulation of egg yolks, thickened by heat and starch, with the acidity of lemon juice preventing the mixture from becoming too cloyingly sweet and also contributing to the setting. The meringue topping is a triumph of aerated protein: whipping egg whites creates a stable foam that traps air, and sugar then stabilizes this foam.
