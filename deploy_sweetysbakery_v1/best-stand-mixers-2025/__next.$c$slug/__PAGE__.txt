        <p>If you are serious about baking, a stand mixer is non-negotiable. It saves your arms, ensures consistent mixing, and opens the door to advanced techniques like brioche and meringue. But with prices ranging from $100 to $800, which one should you buy?</p>
        <h3>1. KitchenAid Artisan Series</h3>
        <p>The gold standard. It features a tilt-head design, 10 speeds, and a dizzying array of colors. It's powerful enough for bread dough but gentle enough for egg whites.</p>
        <h3>2. Kenwood Chef Titanium</h3>
        <p>A beast of a machine. It has a higher wattage motor than the KitchenAid, making it better for heavy, stiff doughs. The bowl illumination is a nice touch.</p>
        <h3>3. Cuisinart Precision Master</h3>
        <p>A fantastic budget-friendly alternative. It offers similar performance to the KitchenAid Classic at a fraction of the price.</p>
        <p><strong>Verdict:</strong> If you want aesthetics and performance, go KitchenAid. If you bake 10 loaves of bread a week, go Kenwood.</p>
