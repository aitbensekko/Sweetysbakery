3:T45f,<h2><a href="/silicone-baking-mats-vs-parchment-paper" style="color: #E65A88; text-decoration: underline;">Silicone Baking Mats vs. Parchment Paper</a></h2>
        <p>Should you invest in a Silpat or stick to the roll of paper? The answer depends on what you are baking.</p>
        <h3>Silicone Mats</h3>
        <p><strong>Pros:</strong> Reusable (eco-friendly), lays flat instantly, nothing sticks to it (great for sugar work).</p>
        <p><strong>Cons:</strong> Can inhibit spreading (cookies might stay too thick), needs to be washed.</p>
        <h3>Parchment Paper</h3>
        <p><strong>Pros:</strong> No cleanup (throw it away), allows cookies to spread naturally, can be cut to size.</p>
        <p><strong>Cons:</strong> Wasteful, can curl up in the oven.</p>
        <p><strong>Verdict:</strong> Use silicone for sugar work and macarons. Use parchment for cookies and cakes.</p>
